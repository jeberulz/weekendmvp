# ship·able Strategy

## Target problem

Design-literate, semi-technical people (designers, design-leaning PMs, indie builders) can see exactly where AI fits a real workflow, but they never ship — they collect tutorials, over-scope, and circle "someday" ideas because they're missing a path, not knowledge. The crux: the moment of *deciding and deploying one small thing live* is the bottleneck, and nothing in their week forces it.

## Our approach

ship·able is a $9, 90-minute live working session that forces the decision and produces a deployed URL on the call — a deliberately tiny, high-trust front door whose real job is to create a qualified buyer list for the $29 DARE Live workshop and, behind it, the high-ticket DARE program.

## Who it's for

**Primary:** The stuck builder-designer — an experienced designer / design-leaning PM / semi-technical indie who's "been meaning to build the AI thing." They're hiring ship·able to stop researching and walk out with one real, live MVP they can show someone this weekend.

**Secondary:** The broad non-technical founder who lands on the public page. They convert on the $9 ticket, but only the DARE-compatible subset is a real backend lead — so paid acquisition is aimed at the primary persona, not this one.

## Key metrics

- **Qualified buyers** — count of $9 ship·able purchasers who match the DARE persona (designer / PM / builder). The real north star; measured in Stripe + a one-field "what do you do" on the Beehiiv capture.
- **Blended CPA** — Meta spend ÷ total $9 buyers. Target ≤ $9 (ticket pays for the click); acceptable to ~$20 because the backend is high-ticket.
- **Live-show rate** — % of buyers who attend live (bonuses are live-only). Target ≥ 40%. Drives DARE conversion.
- **ship·able → DARE conversion** — % of $9 buyers who buy the $29 DARE seat. The whole funnel's validation; target ≥ 15%.
- **Shipped-on-call rate** — % of live attendees who actually deploy a URL. The proof engine for all future marketing; target ≥ 60%.

## Tracks

### Fill the room (Jun 15–27)

Paid + organic acquisition aimed at the primary persona to fill the Jun 27 seats with qualified buyers.

_Why it serves the approach:_ A tiny front door only works if the right people walk through it; this is where buyer-list quality is won or lost.

### Deliver a shippable 90 minutes

The run-of-show, slide deck, Ship Sheet, and live-build demo that get ≥60% of attendees to a deployed URL.

_Why it serves the approach:_ The deployed URL is the product. A weak delivery kills both refunds-risk and the proof we need for everything downstream.

### Bridge to DARE

The same-day → 14-day sequence that turns a $9 buyer into a $29 DARE Live seat and tags high-ticket-program interest.

_Why it serves the approach:_ ship·able is explicitly the front door, not the house. Without an intentional bridge, the loss-leader stays a loss.

- Profit on the $9 ticket — it is a loss leader by design; do not optimize the front door for margin.

## Marketing

**One-liner:** Build and ship a real, live MVP in 90 minutes — for $9, no code, even if you've been sitting on the idea for a year.

**Key message:** The thing keeping you stuck isn't talent or time — it's that you never ship. ship·able forces one small, deployed thing live on the call. You leave with a URL you can send someone today, not another folder of notes.
