# Design With AI — Newsletter Audit & Growth Strategy

Prepared June 16, 2026 · Data pulled live from Beehiiv

---

## The one-line summary

Your list is shrinking, not growing. You have a small, loyal core of about 65 readers who open and click everything, buried inside 406 subscribers, most of whom barely engage. The product is healthy. The engine around it has stalled.

---

## Where you stand today

- **406 active subscribers.** 169 inactive on top of that.
- **662 people have ever joined. 256 left.** Net 406.
- **Last 3 months: 21 joined, 28 left. Net -7.** This is the headline. You are losing subscribers faster than you add them.
- **Open rate 33.8% all-time, 34.7% last 3 months.** Healthy for the inbox, but inflated by Apple privacy opens.
- **Click rate 2.5% all-time, 1.86% last 3 months, and falling.** This is the real engagement number, and it is low.
- **$35.15 earned all-time.** No working monetization.

You launched in December 2024. Eighteen months in, the list is roughly the size of a good single workshop turnout, and it is contracting.

---

## What the data actually shows

### 1. Growth has dried up

New signups slowed to about 7 per month. Almost all of them now come from three API feeds (startup-ideas, shipwithai, shipable). Your website and embed forms, which drove 290+ signups historically (switchtoux.com embed alone brought 108), brought just 2 in the last 3 months.

You are no longer acquiring readers. You are draining a reservoir.

### 2. A loyal core carries the whole list

Your segments tell the real story:

- **Weekend MVP Builders (30 people): 51.5% open, 9.96% click.**
- **Most Active / clickers (65 people): 52.7% open, 8.18% click.**
- **Active subscribers (327 people): 38.9% open, 2.31% click.**
- **Unengaged (93 people): 0% open, 53.8% already unsubscribed.**
- **Inactive 90+ days (121 people): 18% open, flagged for removal.**
- **YabaTech workshop students (118 people): 9.6% open.** Mostly dead weight from a one-time event.

Strip out the dead segments and your true audience is the 65 to 95 people who actually read and click. Everyone else is padding that drags down your sender reputation.

### 3. Clicks are the problem, not opens

Across the last 25 sends: average open 35%, but median click under 2%. Thirteen of those 25 emails got fewer than 2% clicks. Several got zero clicks at all ("A lot has changed," "Build Around the Hook," "Beta access is open").

People open your emails. They rarely do anything after. That is a content-and-CTA problem, not a deliverability problem.

### 4. What gets opened vs. what gets clicked

**Best openers** were specific, numbered, or curiosity-driven:

- "Startup Idea #1: The Finance App Gen Z Actually Opens" — 43.8%
- "5 markets growing faster than AI" — 43.0%
- "AI in UX Design: 10 Game-Changing Strategies" — 42.7%
- "The tool I wish existed when I was job hunting" — 42.4%

**Worst openers** were vague or salesy:

- "Your idea goes live this Saturday ($9)" — 22.2%
- "My Unshakable AI Build Workflow" — 22.0%
- "Stop Overthinking, Start Building" — 25.3%

**Best clickers** had one clear, valuable action:

- "Your beta access is ready" — 9.6% (one access link)
- "22 AI Startup Ideas + Blueprints, free download" — 7.2% (one download)
- "Get Claude Code installed before Thursday" — 5.8% (one install task)
- "AI Feedback Triage Widget" (Idea of the Day) — 4.7%

The pattern is clear. Numbered, concrete subject lines win opens. A single obvious next step wins clicks. Roundups and update emails earn opens but produce no action.

### 5. You have an identity problem

The publication promises "weekly AI-powered design and vibe coding." The actual content spans UX career advice, startup ideas, AI news roundups, build-in-public logs, workshop promos, beta launches, a resume tool, and WeekendMVP. You have relaunched or reset at least three times ("I'm relaunching Design with AI," "A lot has changed," "Long time no talk").

Subscribers signed up for one thing and keep receiving a different thing each week. That mismatch is a churn driver.

### 6. Cadence is inconsistent

You promise weekly. The reality: a two-month silence (Oct to Dec 2025), then a burst of six emails in four days (April 20 to 23), then gaps again. Inboxes reward rhythm. Bursts after silence train people to ignore or unsubscribe.

## What's working (keep these)

- **Deliverability is clean.** 98 to 100% delivery, zero spam reports.
- **The "Idea of the Day" and "Builder Brief" formats** pull above-average clicks when they carry a build link.
- **Your core 65 clickers** are a real asset. They are worth more than the other 340 combined.
- **Specific, numbered subject lines** consistently beat your averages.
- **Single-CTA emails** (download, install, access) drive 5 to 10% clicks, 3x to 5x your norm.

---

## The strategy: fix the engine in four moves

### Move 1 — Pick one promise and hold it (Week 1)

Decide what Design With AI is in one sentence. The data says your strongest pull is "build real AI products on a weekend." Weekend MVP Builders is your best segment by far. Lean into that. Cut UX-career and generic AI-news content, or spin it to a separate list. One audience, one promise, every week.

### Move 2 — Clean the list (Week 1)

- Run a 2-email re-engagement sequence to the 93 unengaged and 121 inactive subscribers ("still want this? click to stay").
- Remove everyone who does not respond.
- Expect the active count to drop below 300. That is good. Your open and click rates will jump, deliverability strengthens, and your metrics finally reflect reality.

Do not fear the smaller number. A list of 250 engaged builders beats 406 ghosts.

### Move 3 — Rebuild the weekly format around one action (Weeks 2 to 4)

Standardize every issue:

- **One numbered, specific subject line.** Test against your winners ("Idea #X," "N tools," "N markets").
- **One core idea or build per email.** Stop bundling.
- **One clear CTA above the fold.** A build link, a download, a reply prompt. Measure clicks, not opens.
- **A reply trigger every issue.** "Reply with X" emails build the relationship and protect deliverability.

Target: lift click rate from 1.9% to 4%+ within 6 weeks.

### Move 4 — Restart acquisition (Weeks 2 to 6)

Your inflow collapsed because the website and embed forms stopped converting. Fix the front door:

- Re-activate the switchtoux.com embed that once drove 108 signups. Confirm it still works.
- Put the "22 AI Startup Ideas" lead magnet (7.2% click, proven) back in front of new traffic as the primary opt-in.
- Turn on Beehiiv recommendations and boosts. You currently get almost nothing from them (3 from designersroll).
- Add a single reader-referral ask to your highest-open issues.

Target: return to net-positive growth (15+ new per month) within 8 weeks.
