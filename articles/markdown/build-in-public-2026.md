# Build in Public in 2026

What's Changed (And What Still Works)

---

Scroll Indie Hackers or X for five minutes and you'll see the same headline: **build in public is dead.**

Founders who used to post daily MRR screenshots have gone quiet. The timeline feels louder with AI-built clones. The engagement bait that worked in 2020 gets three likes in 2026.

So is the strategy over? No. The *performance* version of build in public is over. What replaced it is quieter, slower, and — if you do it right — more durable.

---

## Myth #1: Daily MRR Screenshots Still Work

The 2019 playbook was **performance-in-public** — content optimized for the feed, not for customers.

In 2026, the playbook is **workflow-in-public**: commit-driven updates, weekly demos, monthly retros.

Founders who've crossed meaningful revenue thresholds often dial back financial transparency. Belogubov's widely cited framework: reduce MRR sharing around **$10K/month**, pull back further around **$30K/month**, and shift to "ghost mode" once the product doesn't need timeline validation anymore.

## Myth #2: Build in Public Is Your Distribution Strategy

Posting on X is not a go-to-market plan. It's one channel in a portfolio.

Distribution now looks like a stack: SEO, product-led discovery, micro-communities, partnerships, and — maybe — a consistent public build log.

The X algorithm in 2026 penalizes outbound links heavily. Founders who still grow there lean into a **reply-for-value** ratio — roughly 80% thoughtful replies, 20% original posts.

---

## Myth #3: Your Audience Is Other Indie Hackers

You ship a feature. Indie hackers cheer. Six months later you have a product perfectly tuned for people who will never pay you.

**25%** of Y Combinator's Winter 2025 batch had codebases that were 95% AI-generated — TechCrunch. You're competing for a niche customer's trust, not indie hacker attention.

---

## Myth #4: More Transparency Always Wins

The 2026 rule is **share early, hide later.**

- **Early stage:** Share freely — build log, ugly v1, anonymized interview quotes.
- **Growth ($1K → ~$10K MRR):** Share methodology, not exact metrics.
- **Established (~$10K+ MRR):** Go quiet on numbers or go fully private.

---

## What Still Works in 2026

1. Ship first, narrate second
2. Weekly cadence, not daily noise
3. Reply where buyers already are
4. Stack distribution channels — BiP is channel seven, not channel one
5. Plan for a 6–12 month compounding window

## When to Go Quiet

Go quiet when: you have more inbound than you can handle, competitors are watching your playbook, your customers aren't on social media, or posting is eating shipping hours.

The founders disappearing from your timeline didn't quit. They graduated from audience-building to business-building.

---

## TL;DR

- Build in public isn't dead — **MRR-screenshot theater** is.
- BiP is one distribution channel, not the whole strategy.
- Share early, hide later.
- Build for customers, not for other indie hackers.
