Here's What Nobody Tells You

The startup world loves to say "just start building."

But build what, exactly?

That's the actual blocker.

You're not lazy. You're not unambitious. You're stuck in decision paralysis because you have too many options and no way to pick.

Maybe you've asked ChatGPT for ideas. It spits out a list of 50 "disruptive" concepts. Great. Now you have 50 more things to overthink.

Or you've spent weeks "validating" by reading articles about TAM and market fit. Meanwhile, the weekend passed. And the next one. And the one after that.

The graveyard of side projects isn't filled with bad ideas. It's filled with ideas that were too big, too vague, or never got started at all.

Ready to stop scrolling and start building?

3 Lies About Startup Ideas

Lie #1: "You need a unique, billion-dollar idea"

No, you don't. You need a $1k/month idea first. Something that solves a real pain for a specific person. Something you can actually build. The billion-dollar stuff comes later (if ever). Start small.

Lie #2: "You need to validate for months before building"

Validation paralysis is real. You can spend 3 months surveying people, building spreadsheets, and running "market research." Or you can ship a demo in 48 hours and get real feedback from real people. Which one teaches you more?

Lie #3: "You need a technical co-founder"

Maybe in 2015. Today you have Claude, Cursor, Replit, and a dozen AI tools that can help you build a working demo. You don't need a co-founder. You need a weekend and the right prompts.

Think of It Like a Slot Machine

Most founders fail because they bet everything on ONE idea. They spend months perfecting it, polishing it, dreaming about it. And when it doesn't work? They're crushed.

Smart founders play differently.

They build small. They ship fast. They test multiple ideas. Each weekend MVP is another pull of the lever.

You only need one to hit.
