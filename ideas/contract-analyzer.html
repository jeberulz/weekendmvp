AI Contract Analyzer

Legalese translated to plain English.

The Problem

Nobody reads contracts, but everyone should. Freelancers sign NDAs with crazy non-competes. Founders accept investor terms they don't understand. Employees miss concerning clauses in employment agreements. Lawyers cost $500/hour, so most people just sign and hope for the best.

The Solution

Upload any contract PDF, and AI analyzes it instantly. Get a summary of key terms, warnings about concerning clauses, and plain-English explanations of what you're agreeing to. Know what to negotiate before signing.

AI Prompts to Build This

1. Project Setup

Create a Next.js app for contract analysis. Features: - Upload: PDF drag-drop or paste text - Analysis view: summary, key terms, warnings - Interactive: click on clause to see explanation - History: past analyzed contracts - Export: PDF report of analysis Use pdf.js or similar for PDF text extraction. Add disclaimer: not legal advice, consult a lawyer for important decisions.

2. Contract Analysis

Implement AI contract analysis: Prompt to Claude: "Analyze this contract and provide: 1. SUMMARY: What is this contract about in 2-3 sentences 2. CONTRACT TYPE: Employment, NDA, Service Agreement, etc. 3. KEY TERMS: List the most important obligations and rights 4. RED FLAGS: Highlight concerning clauses like: - Non-compete restrictions - IP assignment clauses - Unlimited liability - Automatic renewal - One-sided termination rights 5. QUESTIONS TO ASK: What should you clarify before signing For each item, explain in plain English what it means for the signer."

3. Interactive Features

Add advanced features: 1. Clause-by-clause: break contract into sections, explain each 2. Compare: upload two versions, show what changed 3. Chat: ask follow-up questions about specific terms 4. Templates: compare against standard versions (e.g., Y Combinator SAFE) 5. Risk score: overall assessment 1-10 with explanation Contract type presets: NDA, employment, freelance, SaaS, investment terms. Show which sections are typical vs unusual for this contract type.
