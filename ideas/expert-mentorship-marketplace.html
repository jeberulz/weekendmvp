Expert Mentorship Marketplace

Curated experts, AI matching, and paid sessions with clean payouts—between free peer lists and $300/mo subscription mentorship.

The Problem

Learners want specific expertise: "How do I price enterprise SaaS as a solo founder?" or "What's the interview loop at Stripe for EM roles?" Generic career coaching does not help. They bounce between free communities (high noise), expensive bootcamps (high commitment), and cold LinkedIn DMs (low response rate).

ADPList proved demand for free mentorship at massive scale—but mentors churn because there is no compensation and mentees struggle to get repeatable access to the same expert. MentorCruise monetizes long-term subscriptions but skews engineering and design. Clarity.fm pioneered per-minute calls yet feels dated. Plato targets engineering managers inside companies, not individuals shopping for experts.

Experts with real credentials want lightweight monetization without building a course. Learners want one great hour, not a twelve-week curriculum. The gap is a marketplace optimized for single-session and micro-package bookings with semantic matching and escrow payouts—see also Virtual Knowledge Hub for the executive-tier variant with async libraries.

The Solution

Experts apply with proof (LinkedIn, portfolio, optional video intro). Learners post a goal or pick a category. The platform embeds expert profiles, runs vector + LLM matching, and surfaces three fits with a one-line "why this expert." Booking via Cal.com embed; payment held until session complete (Stripe Connect). Optional async "written office hours" tier for $49 flat.

MVP loops:

  • Expert onboarding + manual approval (quality gate)
  • Learner goal form → top 3 matches
  • 30/60-min video session + Stripe payout
  • Post-session review + rebook discount
  • Category landing pages for SEO (pricing, PM, fundraising)

Differentiate from Virtual Knowledge Hub by focusing on practitioner experts (IC → senior IC) at $75–$250/session, not VP office hours. Cross-link both properties in Explore More for SEO cluster.

Market Research

Mentorship and coaching platforms collectively exceed $3–5B when you include corporate programs and consumer marketplaces. Post-layoff career anxiety keeps demand counter-cyclical. AI matching is table stakes marketing; real differentiation is supply quality and payout economics experts trust.

  • MentorCruise lists thousands of mentors—validates willingness to pay $100–300/mo for ongoing access.
  • ADPList community scale proves top-of-funnel; monetization layer is the open lane.
  • Expert economy (consulting calls, intro.co) normalized paying for time; Stripe Connect makes marketplace payouts a weekend integration.
  • Category SEO ("find a fundraising mentor") still wins long-tail against generic LinkedIn search.

Stage: proven category, room for niche + better economics. Win a vertical (e.g., B2B SaaS founders) before going horizontal.

AI Prompts to Build This

Copy and paste these into Claude, Cursor, or your favorite AI tool.

1. Marketplace Schema

Create Next.js marketplace: experts (bio, tags, hourly_rate, stripe_account_id, embedding), learners, bookings (scheduled_at, amount_cents, status), reviews. Expert application form with LinkedIn URL + 3 expertise tags. Admin /admin/approve queue. Public /experts grid with filters. Expert profile /experts/[slug] with Cal embed.

2. Semantic Match Endpoint

POST /api/match { goal, background, budget_max }. Embed goal with text-embedding-3-large. Retrieve 20 experts. Claude re-rank to 3 with { expert_id, reason } JSON. Show reasons on UI cards. Log conversions when booking completes.

3. Stripe Connect Escrow

Implement booking checkout: PaymentIntent on platform account with transfer_data.destination = expert Connect id, application_fee_amount = 15%. Webhook payment_intent.succeeded → confirm booking. After session, learner confirms → transfer; auto-release after 72h unless disputed.
