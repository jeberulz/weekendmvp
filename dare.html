You're a strong designer with vague AI ideas… and vague doesn't ship.

You've been paying attention. You've used the tools. You can see, more clearly than most engineers, where AI actually fits a real person's workflow.

And yet nothing's moving. Three traps, usually:

  • The engineer myth. "I need to get technical first." So you collect tutorials instead of making a decision.
  • The big-idea trap. Every idea you take seriously feels like it needs a team and a roadmap. So you take none of them seriously.
  • The model maze. SaaS? Service? Agency? You're trying to pick the perfect vehicle before you've picked the problem.

The hard part of AI products is design judgment — which you already have. What you're missing isn't knowledge. It's a path.

DARE is that path, walked in 90 minutes.

D

Discover. One buyer, one painful workflow.

You already know how to find the real problem under a vague brief — you've done it in every discovery call of your career. We aim that skill at the AI landscape and cut your idea cloud down to one specific person, one workflow, one why-now. Written down.

~25 min
A

Architect. This is where designers win.

Anyone can call an API. Almost nobody can design where the agent acts, where the human stays in control, and why anyone would trust it — that's interaction design, and it's the scarcest skill in AI products right now. You'll map your workflow into an Agent Blueprint: a simple agentic flow a real person would love to use.

~35 min
R+E

Run + Earn. The scrappy v1, and the honest ask.

We scope the smallest real version — live prototype, tiny SaaS, or repeatable service workflow — then turn it into a concrete offer with a price and the exact words to ask for money without cringing. Both land in your 4-week plan: run in weeks 1–3, earn in week 4.

~30 min

Watching other designers circle the same transition, I kept seeing the same three traps — and the same fix. Not more tutorials. A decision, made with a method. DARE is that method: the system I wish someone had handed me at the start, compressed into 90 minutes and applied to your idea, not a hypothetical one.
